Hey! I'm Logan!

Though most people just call me 'Log'!

The Who

I am a recent graduate from Arizona State University with a Bachelor of Science in Graphic Information Technology, focussed on Full Stack Development... Which is just a really long way of saying I like to draw and work with computers. For the last 15 or so years I have been fully invested in anything and everything technology. I guess you could say it's passion! Whether it's videography, building a new system, end-user support, or graphic design I always give it my all. Computers and technology has been a part of my life ever since I was a small... well, smaller, kid. Over the last 10 years I have devled into the world of Graphic Design and Web Development; developing responsive web applications. If I'm not working on a comissioned project, you'll likely find me knee deep in code working on a personal project, drawing, or just gaming online with old friends!

The Spark!

Ever since I was little I can remember being glued to the computer. I spent countless hours online playing popular MMORPGs such as Runescape. Since we're being honest, I still play too much Runescape. At six years old I remember surfing the web for various videogame guides, playthroughs, other games that I could play... anything related to the games I was interested in at the time. Being online since I young age has given me front row access to the ever changing landscape that is the internet. As I grew I started to become more interested in the how and the why of programs.

A Life Long Endevour

I disctinctly remember when I was around 8 years old being shown AOL mail for the first time. My Mother had called me downstairs to the family computer and proceeded to type out a short message and click the send button. At the time I had absolutely no idea what was going on. Not 2 minutes later we heard the traditional 'You got mail!' tone from AOL, and saw a new message in my mother's inbox. Opening the message revealed a response to my mother's origional message from my Grandmother who lived a few blocks down the street. I was absolutely flabbergasted. Sure, I had spent more hours than any kid should have on the computer, talking with friends in real time on the game, but I had never put it together that I could use this technology to talk with friends and family that didn't play videogames online.

Shortly after this interaction with AOL mail I became more interested in how exactly email and the internet worked. How was it that I could send a message into the void and receive a response almost instantly? How does running an installation disk instal a program on your computer? I was determined to find out, some way or another. A few years later my older brother had bought his first 'gaming' pc. As his little brother, I just had to have one for myself. So, over the following months I saved up for my own. With 500$ in hand we made a trip to the local Fry's Electronics and put together a muppit of a computer, but it was my first and I enjoyed every minute of that agonizing weekend putting it together. And as the saying goes, the rest is history.

Creative Beginnings

In highschool I was never much of an artistic kid. I took drama class, but art was not on the tables for me. However, during my senior year two of my close friends took the class and I became hooked. At the time I would go home from school and spend hours learning to draw; even tracing pictures to make myself feel better. Little did I know, this small habbit would shape the future of my college career.

During my first year of college I studied at Colorado State Univeristy as a Graphic Design major. Being so far from home and trying to learn a new skill was very difficult for me and I ended up moving back in with my parents. That being said, I had no intention of giving up on my dream. The following year I started attending Arizona State Univeristy's online course for Graphic Design. Through a chance conversation with my old Colorado roomate, I discovered my love of coding. That friend ended up pushing me to following my interestes, and as a result I started my new major in Graphic Information Technology.

From Design to Development

Although I started as a Graphic Design major, I was easily able to transition into Graphic Information Technology. Applying my ever expanding design skills to my code I was able to expand my horizons by learning how to not only design responsive webpages, but also how to hand code those pages from scratch. During my studies I learned how to create responsive, interactive, and applealing web applications with intuative user design.
